Cold Brew

Cold Brews just take a lot of patience and forethought. If made right they have unique flavors and even give a base for iced lattes. Still tasty even on a cold day.


Instructions

  • 1. Pick Coffee
    For cold brews you will want a medium-dark roast for the extra strength and boldness. This is because when you're brewing, you are actually making concentrate and then will add water/milk to it so the stronger the concentrate is the better your cup will taste.
  • 2. Pick container
    For brewing you can really brew in any container that will hold about 16 cups of water. When I started I would brew in my french press.
  • 3. Grind coffee
    For coldbrews you will want to get an even more coarse grind than chemex, similar to sea salt. 340 Grams total.
  • 4. Pour One
    First pour 1 cup of water into your container. Dump in 170 grams of coffee. Pour in 3 more cups of water. Stir. Wait 5 minutes.
  • 5. Pour Two
    Second dump remaining 170 grams of coffee into container. Pour in 3 cups of water. Stir.
  • 6. Store
    Ideally you will store and let brew 20-23 hours in your fridge. If it won't fit then you can just put it in a dark cool place for 20-23 hours.
  • 7. Strain
    You will want a very small strainer that can seperate small particles. Pour your container of cold brew through strainer into a storage container, preferrably glass.
  • 8. Make A Drink
    Like we said earlier, you are making concentrate. So next you will have to trial and error to find what you like but I typically do a 1:1 ratio of coffee to water. You can also do 1:closeto1 and add in some milk if you like that in your coffee.
  • 9. Lattes
    Next you can mix it up with some simple syrup recipes like vanilla or brown sugar. Try to mix 1/4 C Milk with 3/4 C Concentrate and 3 T Vanilla simple syrup for a tasty iced latte.
